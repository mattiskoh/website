3D Printed hand prosthesis

Design and fabrication of 3D printed pneumatic-actuated hand prosthetic (Spring 2015)

An Honors research project done at the TU Delft, this extracurricular research project focused on developing a simple prototype of a 3D printed hand prosthetic. The main objective of the project was to develop a simple hand prosthesis that uses a pneumatic actuator for its actuation. By experimenting with additive manufacturing techniques the goal was to create a low tech system that could easily be customized and that our team could fabricate ourselves. The project started with an in-depth literature research to find the basic design and dimensions for the prosthesis. The CAD model was made using Solidworks and optimized to be printed with a single material Makerbot.

Together with Jozef Stuijt & Yannick Chance
Supervised by Dr. Ir. Dick Plettenburg
