Internship MX3D

Developing a positioning system for the robotic metal 3D printing (Fall 2016)

The main focus of the internship was to develop a positioning system for MX3D's proprietary robotic metal 3D printing process. This system, that is to be used for the Bridge Project, allows for printing out-of-the-box, i.e. parts that are larger than the robot's working range by repositioning the robot in space. The final positioning system used a Leica total station and the positioning procedure was developed using ABB Robotstudio, Rhino and Grasshopper. The procedure was successfully implemented and achieved an accuracy of a few millimeters allowing for seamless prints of large parts.

An important focus of this internship was combining software and design with hardware and manufacturing. Some other side-projects I worked on during my time at MX3D:

  • Point distribution for multi-walled curves [Software, Python]
  • Crystalline point distribution for solids [Software, C#]
  • Test printing multi-walled pipes [Fabrication]
  • Designing and fabricating custom robot components [Mechanical design]
